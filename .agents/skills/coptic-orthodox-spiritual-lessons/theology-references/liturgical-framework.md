# Liturgical and Sacramental Framework

This file maps the Coptic Orthodox liturgical year, major feasts, fasts, and
sacramental life so that lesson content can be grounded in the Church's rhythm
rather than treated as standalone topics.

---

## The Liturgical Year Overview

The Coptic year is structured around two axes:
1. **The festal cycle** - fixed feasts commemorating events in salvation history
2. **The fasting cycle** - periods of fasting that prepare the soul and mark the year

Content should be aware of where a lesson falls in this rhythm. A lesson on
repentance lands differently during the Great Lent than during Pentecost season.

---

## Major Fasts

| Fast | Duration | Season | Spiritual Character |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Great Lent (Kiahk + Lent)** | 55 days | Before Pascha | Deepest fast. Repentance, mourning for sin, preparation for Pascha. Most serious content season. |
| **Apostles' Fast** | Variable (15-49 days) | After Pentecost | Commemorates apostles' fast before their mission. Good season for calling, vocation, witness. |
| **Fast of the Virgin Mary** | 15 days (Aug 1-15) | Summer | Devotion to the Theotokos. Intercession, holiness, the Dormition. |
| **Advent Fast (Kiahk)** | 43 days | Before Christmas | Anticipation, prophecy, preparation. Strong OT content season. |
| **Fast of Nineveh** | 3 days | Before Lent | Repentance and divine mercy. Jonah. Very focused fast. |
| **Wednesday and Friday** | Weekly | Year-round | Wednesday: betrayal. Friday: Crucifixion. Ongoing weekly ascetic rhythm. |

## Major Feasts

### The Seven Major Feasts of the Lord

| Feast | Date (Coptic) | Theme for Lessons |
|---|---|---|
| **Annunciation** | 29 Baramhat | Incarnation begins. The *Theotokos*. God enters time. |
| **Palm Sunday** | Variable | Kingship of Christ. Crowd's misunderstanding. What kind of king? |
| **Pascha (Easter)** | Variable | Resurrection. Death conquered. The center of all things. |
| **Ascension** | 40 days after Pascha | Christ takes our humanity to the Father. What He ascended with. |
| **Pentecost** | 50 days after Pascha | The Holy Spirit. The Church born. Mission and charism. |
| **Transfiguration** | 13 Mesore | Theosis revealed. The uncreated light. Moses and Elijah. |
| **Nativity (Christmas)** | 29 Kiahk | Incarnation. God becomes man. The kenosis. Emmanuel. |

### Other Significant Feasts

| Feast | Date | Theme |
|---|---|---|
| **Epiphany / Theophany** | 11 Tobe | Baptism of Christ. Trinity revealed. Sanctification of water. |
| **Entry into Egypt** | 24 Bashans | Holy Family. Egypt as type. Fulfillment of Hosea 11:1. |
| **Feast of the Cross** | 17 Tote / 10 Baramhat | The Cross exalted. Typology. Helen and Constantine. |
| **Feast of St. Mark** | 30 Baramhat | Apostolic foundation of the Coptic Church. |

## Holy Week

Holy Week is the liturgical climax of the Coptic year. Every day has distinct services,
readings, and spiritual character. Content during or about Holy Week should reflect this.

| Day | Focus | Key Services |
|---|---|---|
| **Palm Sunday** | Triumphal entry. False expectations of kingship. | Palms liturgy |
| **Monday** | Cursing of the fig tree. Spiritual fruitfulness. | |
| **Tuesday** | Hard sayings. Parables of judgment. End times. | |
| **Wednesday** | The betrayal. Judas. The anointing at Bethany. | |
| **Covenant Thursday** | The Last Supper. Washing of feet. Institution of Eucharist. | Foot washing rite, Liturgy of the Covenant |
| **Good Friday** | The Passion. The Cross. The seven last words. | The 12 Passion Gospel readings, Vespers |
| **Holy Saturday** | Waiting. Burial. Descent into Hades. | Saturday morning liturgy |
| **Resurrection Liturgy** | Pascha. The empty tomb. | Late Saturday night into Sunday |

## The Sacraments (Mysteries)

The seven sacraments of the Coptic Orthodox Church are entry points for connecting
any lesson to the sacramental life. Use them not as theological footnotes but as
living realities the hearer participates in.

| Sacrament | Connection to lesson content |
|---|---|
| **Baptism** | New birth, death to sin, putting on Christ, adoption |
| **Chrismation (Myron)** | Sealing of the Holy Spirit, anointing as prophet/priest/king |
| **Eucharist** | Union with Christ, the Incarnation continued, source and summit of the Church's life |
| **Confession (Repentance)** | Ongoing healing, the returning prodigal, the physician of souls |
| **Unction (Holy Oil)** | Healing of body and soul, the elders of James 5 |
| **Marriage** | Icon of Christ and the Church, covenant love, Cana |
| **Holy Orders** | Apostolic succession, service, the shepherd |

## The Agpeya (Canonical Hours)

The Agpeya structures daily prayer into seven hours based on the Psalms. Lesson
content can reference these hours to connect Scripture study to the hearer's
existing prayer life.

| Hour | Time | Theme |
|---|---|---|
| **Prime (First Hour)** | 6:00 AM | Sunrise, the light of Christ, thanksgiving for new day |
| **Terce (Third Hour)** | 9:00 AM | The Holy Spirit descends at Pentecost |
| **Sext (Sixth Hour)** | 12:00 PM | The Crucifixion. Midday darkness. |
| **None (Ninth Hour)** | 3:00 PM | Christ's death on the Cross. The thief. |
| **Vespers (Eleventh Hour)** | Sunset | The descent from the Cross. Joseph of Arimathea. |
| **Compline (Twelfth Hour)** | Bedtime | Entrusting the soul to God. Nunc Dimittis. |
| **Midnight Praise** | Midnight | The wise virgins. The Bridegroom comes. Watchfulness. |

## Connecting Lessons to the Liturgical Calendar

When preparing content, ask:

1. **Where are we in the year?** Lent calls for repentance content. Pascha season calls for resurrection and newness. Kiahk calls for typology and prophecy.

2. **Is there a feast this week?** A lesson near the Feast of the Cross naturally opens to typology and the exaltation of Christ's suffering. A lesson near Pentecost opens to the Holy Spirit, the Church, and charism.

3. **What is the congregation fasting?** Content prepared during a fast should acknowledge and enrich the fast, not ignore it.

4. **What readings is the congregation hearing at liturgy?** If the Sunday lectionary covers a passage related to the lesson, make that connection explicit. It anchors the teaching in the Church's communal life.
