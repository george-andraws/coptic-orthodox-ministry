## Introduction

This lesson was prepared for a barbecue / fellowship meeting setting — a shorter, more conversational format. The central theme: **God sees every individual, and so should we.**

---

## The Lost Sheep (Luke 15:3–7)

> "What man of you, having a hundred sheep, if he has lost one of them, does not leave the ninety-nine in the open country, and go after the one that is lost, until he finds it?"
> — Luke 15:4

Key observations:
- The shepherd leaves the **ninety-nine** for the **one**
- He does not send others — he **goes himself**
- He **carries** it back — it cannot walk on its own
- Heaven rejoices over **one** sinner who repents

The mathematics of God's love: **the one is as precious as the ninety-nine.**

## God Sees the Individual

### The Samaritan Woman (John 4)

- She came to the well **alone**, at the sixth hour (midday) — avoiding other women
- Jesus was waiting for her — alone, tired, by the well
- He asked her about her deepest wound: "Go, call your husband" (John 4:16)
- She became the first missionary: "Come, see a man who told me all that I ever did" (John 4:29)

God did not reach Samaria through a campaign. He reached it through **one woman**, met at a well.

### The Sinful Woman (Luke 7:36–50)

- She entered a Pharisee's house — uninvited, unwelcome
- She wept at Jesus' feet, dried them with her hair, anointed them with costly perfume
- The Pharisee saw a sinner. Jesus saw a person who loved much.

> "Therefore I tell you, her sins, which are many, are forgiven — for she loved much."
> — Luke 7:47

### Moses and the Burning Bush (Exodus 3)

- Moses was alone, in the wilderness, tending sheep
- He turned aside to see a strange sight
- God called him **by name**: "Moses, Moses!" (Exodus 3:4)

God's revelation of Himself — "I AM WHO I AM" — came to **one man**, in a desert, at a burning bush.

## Our Call: Connect with Each Person

If God cares for each person individually, then we — made in His image — are called to do the same in our community.

### Practical Assignment

At the next fellowship or gathering:

1. **Do not stay in your comfort group** — resist the pull to spend the whole evening with the people you already know
2. **Find the person standing alone** — there is always someone
3. **Ask one real question** and actually listen to the answer
4. **Remember one thing** about them for next time

> "The ninety-nine are fine. Go find the one."

---

## Reflection Questions

1. When has someone sought you out individually, and what did it mean to you?
2. Is there someone in our community you see but don't know? What's one step you can take?
3. How does knowing that God pursued you individually change the way you see yourself?
