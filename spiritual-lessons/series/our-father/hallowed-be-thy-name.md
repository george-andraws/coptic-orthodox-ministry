## The Lord's Prayer: The First Petition

After addressing God as "Our Father in heaven," the first petition of the Lord's Prayer is:

> "Hallowed be Your name."
> — Matthew 6:9

Before asking for bread, forgiveness, or deliverance from evil, we pray for the hallowing of God's name. This is not an accident of ordering — it reveals what should be first in the Christian's heart.

---

## Greek Exegesis: ἁγιασθήτω (Hagiasthētō)

The word translated "hallowed" is the Greek **ἁγιασθήτω** (*hagiasthētō*):

- Root: **ἅγιος** (*hagios*) — holy, set apart, sacred
- Verb form: aorist passive imperative — "let it be made holy"
- The **passive** voice is significant: we do not make God's name holy. God's name is intrinsically holy. We pray that it would be *treated as holy*, *acknowledged as holy*, *manifested as holy* — in us, through us, and around us.

So the petition means: "Let Your name be recognized and honored as the holy thing it truly is."

---

## Why Is a Name So Important?

In Hebrew culture, a name is not merely a label — it is the revelation of character, identity, and authority.

- **Adam** named the animals, which meant he understood their nature and had authority over them
- When God changed a person's name, He changed their identity: **Abram → Abraham**, **Jacob → Israel**, **Simon → Peter**
- To invoke someone's name is to invoke their presence and power

God's name is His self-disclosure — it is how He has chosen to reveal Himself to us. To "hallow" His name is to respond to His self-revelation with the reverence it deserves.

---

## How God Has Revealed His Name

### 1. "I AM WHO I AM" — Exodus 3:14

> "God said to Moses, 'I AM WHO I AM.' And he said, 'Say this to the people of Israel: I AM has sent me to you.'"
> — Exodus 3:14

The divine name **YHWH** (from the Hebrew *hayah* — "to be") is the most holy name in Judaism. It is the name of the One who simply **is** — uncaused, self-existent, eternal.

### 2. "The LORD of Hosts" — Isaiah 6:3

> "Holy, holy, holy is the LORD of hosts; the whole earth is full of his glory!"
> — Isaiah 6:3

The seraphim hallow God's name in the heavenly liturgy. The thrice-holy (*Trisagion*) is the model for all human worship.

### 3. "My Name Will Be Great Among the Nations" — Malachi 1:11

> "For from the rising of the sun to its setting my name will be great among the nations, and in every place incense will be offered to my name, and a pure offering."
> — Malachi 1:11

The hallowing of God's name is a global, eschatological project — it will be accomplished when all nations worship the one true God.

### 4. "Father, glorify Your name" — John 12:28

> "Father, glorify your name." Then a voice came from heaven: "I have glorified it, and I will glorify it again."
> — John 12:28

Jesus Himself prays for the hallowing of the Father's name. And the Father responds: it has been glorified and will be glorified again — through the Cross and Resurrection.

---

## How Jesus Glorifies the Father's Name

> "I have manifested your name to the people whom you gave me out of the world."
> — John 17:6

> "I made known to them your name, and I will continue to make it known, that the love with which you have loved me may be in them, and I in them."
> — John 17:26

Jesus glorifies the Father's name by:
1. **Revealing His character** — "He who has seen me has seen the Father" (John 14:9)
2. **Living in perfect obedience** — "I always do the things that are pleasing to him" (John 8:29)
3. **The Cross** — "Now is the Son of Man glorified, and God is glorified in him" (John 13:31)

---

## How We Hallow God's Name

We hallow God's name not primarily by saying the right words, but by living in a way that honors who God is:

> "Let your light shine before others, so that they may see your good works and give glory to your Father who is in heaven."
> — Matthew 5:16

We profane His name by:
- Living in ways that contradict the Gospel
- Bearing the name "Christian" while practicing cruelty, dishonesty, or lovelessness

We hallow His name by:
- Living visibly and joyfully as His children
- Treating others with the dignity that reflects the image of God in them
- Worshiping with reverence and understanding
- Praying, fasting, and giving — which Jesus assumes in Matthew 6

## Patristic Commentary

> "When we pray 'Hallowed be Your name,' we are not praying that God becomes holy — He is always holy. We are praying that we become holy, that His holiness may shine through us."
> — St. Cyprian of Carthage

> "To hallow the name of God is to contemplate His glory with reverence and to respond to that glory with a life worthy of the name we bear."
> — St. Cyril of Jerusalem

> "The name of God is hallowed when we who bear His name live in such a way that those around us see His light."
> — St. John Chrysostom

## Practical Steps

1. **Before you say God's name** — in prayer, in conversation, in teaching — pause. Remember that this is the name above every name (Phil 2:9).
2. **Examine how you bear the name "Christian."** Does your life hallow or profane it?
3. **Pray the Trisagion with attention:** "Holy God, Holy Mighty, Holy Immortal — have mercy on us." Let these words move from the lips to the heart.
4. **Ask yourself this week:** In what one area of my life does God's name need to be more hallowed by my actions?
