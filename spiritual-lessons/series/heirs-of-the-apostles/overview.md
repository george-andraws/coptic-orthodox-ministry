## Series Goals

- Introduce the Church Fathers as living witnesses, not historical relics
- Show how each Father responded to the theological and spiritual crises of his age
- Draw practical applications from their lives for modern Orthodox Christians
- Demonstrate the continuity of the Apostolic faith from the Apostles to today

---

## What Does "Heirs of the Apostles" Mean?

The Church Fathers did not invent new theology. They received the faith "once for all delivered to the saints" (Jude 1:3) and defended, articulated, and handed it on. They are heirs — not innovators.

> "The tradition of the Apostles has been made manifest in the whole world, and it is possible for every person who wishes to see the truth to contemplate the tradition of the Apostles clearly, in every church throughout the whole world."
> — St. Irenaeus of Lyons, *Against Heresies* III.3

## How to Engage This Series

1. Come prepared to read a short primary text each week
2. Ask: *What crisis did this Father face? How does it mirror our age?*
3. Identify one practical lesson from each Father's life
4. Pray for the intercession of each Father before the session
