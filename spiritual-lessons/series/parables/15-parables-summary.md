## Introduction

An English translation and summary of Pope Shenouda III's book *"15 Parables Said by Our Lord Jesus Christ"*, supplemented with contextual summaries and lessons for each parable.

---

## What Is a Parable?

A parable (Greek: *parabolē*) is a short story drawn from ordinary life that illuminates a spiritual truth. Jesus used parables for two purposes (Matt 13:10–17):

1. To **reveal** truth to those with open hearts: "To you it has been given to know the secrets of the kingdom"
2. To **conceal** truth from those who have closed their hearts: "seeing they do not see, and hearing they do not hear"

## The 15 Parables

### 1. The Sower (Matthew 13:3–9, 18–23)

**Summary:** A sower scatters seed on four types of soil — the path, rocky ground, thorns, and good soil. Only the last bears fruit.

**Lesson:** The condition of our heart determines whether God's Word takes root and bears fruit. We are responsible for the soil of our hearts.

---

### 2. The Tares (Wheat and Weeds) (Matthew 13:24–30, 36–43)

**Summary:** An enemy sows weeds among wheat. The master tells servants to let both grow together until harvest, when the separation will be made.

**Lesson:** The Church contains both genuine and false disciples. The final judgment belongs to God alone. Do not presume to separate before the harvest.

---

### 3. The Mustard Seed (Matthew 13:31–32)

**Summary:** The smallest of seeds grows into the largest of garden plants, where birds nest in its branches.

**Lesson:** The Kingdom of God begins invisibly small (a manger, a cross, twelve fishermen) but grows to encompass the nations. Faith as small as a mustard seed can move mountains.

---

### 4. The Leaven (Matthew 13:33)

**Summary:** A woman hides a small amount of leaven in a large batch of flour until all of it is leavened.

**Lesson:** The Gospel transforms from within, gradually and invisibly. One holy life leavens a family; one faithful church leavens a community.

---

### 5. The Hidden Treasure (Matthew 13:44)

**Summary:** A man finds treasure hidden in a field, then joyfully sells everything to buy the field.

**Lesson:** The Kingdom of God is worth more than everything we have. The joy of discovery motivates total surrender — not reluctant sacrifice.

---

### 6. The Pearl of Great Price (Matthew 13:45–46)

**Summary:** A merchant seeking fine pearls finds one of surpassing value and sells everything to buy it.

**Lesson:** Some people stumble upon the Kingdom (hidden treasure); others spend their lives searching for it (the merchant). Both, on finding it, give up everything — and consider it a gain, not a loss.

---

### 7. The Dragnet (Matthew 13:47–50)

**Summary:** A net cast into the sea gathers fish of every kind; at the shore, the good are sorted from the bad.

**Lesson:** The Church gathers all kinds. The final sorting is God's work, at the end of the age. Until then, preach to all without prejudging.

---

### 8. The Lost Sheep (Luke 15:3–7)

**Summary:** A shepherd leaves ninety-nine sheep to search for one that is lost, and rejoices more over that one than over the ninety-nine.

**Lesson:** God's love is not proportional — it is total. He pursues each person individually, not as a statistical aggregate.

---

### 9. The Prodigal Son (Luke 15:11–32)

**Summary:** A son takes his inheritance, wastes it, and returns in repentance. The father runs to meet him, restores him fully, and holds a feast.

**Lesson:** Repentance is always received with joy. The father does not wait for a full apology — he runs at the first sign of return. The elder son warns against the pride of external religion without inner love.

---

### 10. The Good Samaritan (Luke 10:25–37)

**Summary:** A man beaten by robbers is ignored by religious leaders but helped by a despised Samaritan.

**Lesson:** Love has no ethnic or religious limits. True mercy crosses social boundaries. "Go and do likewise."

---

### 11. The Talents (Matthew 25:14–30)

**Summary:** A master distributes talents to three servants according to their ability. Two invest and double them; one hides his talent and is condemned.

**Lesson:** God gives each person gifts according to their ability — not equally, but proportionally. He expects a return on the investment. Burying one's gifts out of fear is not humility — it is unfaithfulness.

---

### 12. The Ten Virgins (Matthew 25:1–13)

**Summary:** Ten virgins await the bridegroom. Five bring extra oil; five do not. The bridegroom is delayed; the unprepared five go to buy oil and miss the wedding.

**Lesson:** Spiritual readiness cannot be borrowed at the last moment. The oil of grace, prayer, and virtue must be accumulated over a lifetime.

---

### 13. The Pharisee and the Tax Collector (Luke 18:9–14)

**Summary:** A Pharisee prays with self-congratulation; a tax collector beats his breast and asks for mercy. The second goes home justified.

**Lesson:** God humbles the proud and exalts the humble. Prayer that is truly heard begins with poverty of spirit, not a list of achievements.

---

### 14. The Rich Man and Lazarus (Luke 16:19–31)

**Summary:** A rich man feasts daily; poor Lazarus lies at his gate. After death their positions are reversed. The rich man in torment asks for his brothers to be warned — but Moses and the Prophets are sufficient.

**Lesson:** The choices of this life have eternal consequences. Indifference to suffering is not a neutral position. Scripture is sufficient warning — a miracle would not convince those who refuse to hear.

---

### 15. The Workers in the Vineyard (Matthew 20:1–16)

**Summary:** A vineyard owner hires workers at different hours of the day, then pays all of them the same wage. The early workers grumble.

**Lesson:** God's grace is not earned by duration of service. The thief on the cross, the deathbed convert, and the lifelong monk receive the same eternal life. This is not injustice — it is grace. Grace exceeds merit.

---

## Conclusion

> "All these things Jesus said to the crowds in parables; indeed, he said nothing to them without a parable. This was to fulfill what was spoken by the prophet: 'I will open my mouth in parables; I will utter what has been hidden since the foundation of the world.'"
> — Matthew 13:34–35

Pope Shenouda III observed that these parables cover every dimension of the Christian life: the Kingdom, the heart, judgment, grace, repentance, prayer, stewardship, and the afterlife. Taken together, they are a complete catechism in story form.
