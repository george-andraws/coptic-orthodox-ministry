## The Text (Matthew 25:14–30)

> "For it will be like a man going on a journey, who called his servants and entrusted to them his property. To one he gave five talents, to another two, to another one, to each according to his ability. Then he went away."
> — Matthew 25:14–15

> "Now after a long time the master of those servants came and settled accounts with them. And he who had received the five talents came forward, bringing five talents more, saying, 'Master, you delivered to me five talents; here, I have made five talents more.' His master said to him, 'Well done, good and faithful servant. You have been faithful over a little; I will set you over much. Enter into the joy of your master.'"
> — Matthew 25:19–21

> "He also who had received the one talent came forward, saying, 'Master, I knew you to be a hard man, reaping where you did not sow, and gathering where you scattered no seed, so I was afraid, and I went and hid your talent in the ground. Here, you have what is yours.'"
> — Matthew 25:24–25

> "But his master answered him, 'You wicked and slothful servant! You knew that I reap where I have not sown and gather where I scattered no seed? Then you ought to have invested my money with the bankers, and at my return I would have received what was my own with interest. So take the talent from him and give it to him who has the ten talents.'"
> — Matthew 25:26–28

## Background: The Weight of a Talent

A **talent** was not a coin — it was a unit of weight, equivalent to approximately **75 pounds of silver**. In the first century, one talent was worth approximately **20 years of a laborer's wages**.

- One talent = 20 years of work
- Five talents = 100 years of work
- Two talents = 40 years of work

This is not a story about pocket change. The master entrusted enormous wealth to his servants. The stewardship of these gifts is a serious and weighty matter.

## General Interpretive Approach

### The Talents as Gifts from God

The talents represent everything God has entrusted to us:
- Spiritual gifts (1 Corinthians 12)
- Natural abilities and talents
- Time
- Money and material resources
- Relationships and influence
- The Faith itself — the deposit of truth entrusted to the Church

### The Distribution: Why Unequally?

> "To each according to his ability."
> — Matthew 25:15

This is perhaps the most important phrase in the parable. The master did not give equally — he gave according to capacity. The one-talent servant received what he was able to handle.

**Common objection:** "Why would God give less to some than to others?"

**Answer:** The distribution is not about favor — it is about **fitness**. The one who receives five has been prepared to manage five. Greater gifts come with greater responsibility:

> "Everyone to whom much was given, of him much will be required."
> — Luke 12:48

The one-talent servant was not treated unjustly. He was given exactly what he was ready for. His sin was not receiving little — it was refusing to use what he received.

### Why Take from the Poor and Give to the Rich?

> "For to everyone who has, more will be given, and he will have an abundance. But from the one who has not, even what he has will be taken away."
> — Matthew 25:29

This seems unfair. But the logic is:
- The servant who buried his talent was treating it as if he had nothing
- He refused the increase that stewardship would have brought
- Therefore, even what he appeared to have was functionally nothing

This is a law of the spiritual life: gifts not used atrophy. Faith not exercised weakens. Love not expressed cools.

## Application: Service, Eucharist, Family

### Service

What gift has God given you that you are using in service to others? What gift are you burying — out of fear, laziness, or comparison to others who have more?

### Eucharist

The Eucharist is the greatest gift entrusted to the Church — the Body and Blood of Christ. How are we "investing" this gift? Are we receiving it and allowing it to produce fruit in our lives?

### Family

Children are the talents God entrusts to parents. The question at the accounting: what did you do with the souls entrusted to you?

## Discussion Questions

1. If your life were the parable, which servant are you — and why?
2. What gift are you most tempted to bury? What is the fear underneath?
3. What does "doubling the talent" look like concretely in your current season of life?
4. How do you understand the equal reward ("Enter into the joy of your master") given to both the five-talent and two-talent servants?
