## Introduction

Why did God foretell the Cross centuries before it happened? Because the Incarnation and the Passion are not accidents of history — they are the eternal plan of God for our salvation. The Old Testament is full of prophecies, types, and shadows pointing directly to the suffering, death, and resurrection of the Messiah.

---

## Isaiah 52:13 – 53:12: The Suffering Servant

> "He was despised and rejected by men, a man of sorrows and acquainted with grief; and as one from whom men hide their faces he was despised, and we esteemed him not."
> — Isaiah 53:3

> "But he was pierced for our transgressions; he was crushed for our iniquities; upon him was the chastisement that brought us peace, and with his wounds we are healed."
> — Isaiah 53:5

### Fulfillment Table: Isaiah 53

| Prophecy (Isaiah 53) | Fulfillment (New Testament) |
|---------------------|----------------------------|
| "He was despised and rejected" (v. 3) | John 1:11 — "His own people did not receive him" |
| "He bore our griefs and carried our sorrows" (v. 4) | Matt 8:17 — He healed all who were sick |
| "He was led like a lamb to the slaughter" (v. 7) | Mark 15:5 — "Jesus made no further answer" |
| "They made his grave with the wicked" (v. 9) | Matt 27:38 — Crucified between two thieves |
| "He poured out his soul to death" (v. 12) | Luke 23:46 — "Father, into your hands I commit my spirit" |

## Psalm 22: The Cry from the Cross

> "My God, my God, why have you forsaken me? Why are you so far from saving me?"
> — Psalm 22:1

This Psalm, written by David approximately 1000 BC, describes the Crucifixion in vivid detail — centuries before crucifixion was even practiced.

### Fulfillment Table: Psalm 22

| Prophecy (Psalm 22) | Fulfillment |
|--------------------|-------------|
| "All who see me mock me; they make mouths at me" (v. 7) | Matt 27:39–44 — passersby derided him |
| "They divide my garments among them, and for my clothing they cast lots" (v. 18) | John 19:24 — soldiers cast lots for his tunic |
| "They have pierced my hands and feet" (v. 16) | John 20:27 — Thomas touches the nail marks |
| "I am poured out like water, and all my bones are out of joint" (v. 14) | John 19:34 — blood and water from his side |
| "You who fear the LORD, praise him!" (v. 23) | The Resurrection — the psalm ends in praise |

## The Passover Lamb (Exodus 12)

> "Your lamb shall be without blemish, a male a year old... and the whole assembly of the congregation of Israel shall kill their lambs at twilight."
> — Exodus 12:5–6

> "They shall eat the flesh that night, roasted on fire... with unleavened bread and bitter herbs they shall eat it."
> — Exodus 12:8

Christ is the true Passover Lamb:

- **Without blemish** — "in him there is no sin" (1 John 3:5)
- **Blood on the doorposts** — the Cross, which delivers from the angel of death
- **Not a bone broken** (Exodus 12:46) — John 19:36 — "Not one of his bones will be broken"
- **Eaten** — the Eucharist: "Take, eat; this is my body" (Matt 26:26)

## The Sign of Jonah

> "For just as Jonah was three days and three nights in the belly of the great fish, so will the Son of Man be three days and three nights in the heart of the earth."
> — Matthew 12:40

Jonah was:
- Cast into the sea (death) to save the sailors
- In the belly of the fish for three days (burial)
- Vomited onto dry land (resurrection)
- Sent to the Gentiles (the Church's mission to all nations)

## The Bronze Serpent (Numbers 21:4–9)

> "And as Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, so must the Son of Man be lifted up, that whoever believes in him may have eternal life."
> — John 3:14–15

- The Israelites were bitten by serpents (sin and death)
- Moses made a bronze serpent and lifted it on a pole
- All who **looked** at it lived
- Christ was lifted up on the Cross — all who look to him in faith receive eternal life

## Why Did God Reveal the Cross in Advance?

1. **To confirm that the Cross was not a defeat** — it was the eternal plan of God, not an accident
2. **To prepare a people who could recognize their Messiah** — the disciples understood the Scriptures only after the Resurrection (Luke 24:27)
3. **To show that salvation always came through sacrifice** — from Abel's lamb to the Passover to the Temple, God was teaching the logic of the Cross
4. **To demonstrate the unity of the two Testaments** — the Old is the New concealed; the New is the Old revealed

## Spiritual Application

The Cross is not a moment in history that we observe from a distance. We participate in it:

> "We were buried therefore with him by baptism into death, in order that, just as Christ was raised from the dead by the glory of the Father, we too might walk in newness of life."
> — Romans 6:4

**Questions for reflection:**
1. When you suffer, do you see your suffering as purposeless or as sharing in Christ's Cross?
2. How does knowing that the Cross was planned from eternity affect your trust in God during trials?
3. Which Old Testament type (Passover, Jonah, Bronze Serpent) speaks most clearly to your life right now? Why?
