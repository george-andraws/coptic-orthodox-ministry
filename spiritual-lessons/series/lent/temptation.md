## Introduction

The temptation of Jesus in the wilderness is not just a story about what Jesus resisted — it is a template for how we are to resist temptation. Christ faced everything the enemy brings, and He overcame. By being united to Him, we overcome in Him.

> "For we do not have a high priest who is unable to sympathize with our weaknesses, but one who in every respect has been tempted as we are, yet without sin."
> — Hebrews 4:15

---

## The Three Temptations (Matthew 4:1–11)

### 1. The Temptation of the Flesh: Turn These Stones to Bread

> "And the tempter came and said to him, 'If you are the Son of God, command these stones to become loaves of bread.'"
> — Matthew 4:3

**After forty days of fasting**, Jesus was hungry. The temptation targeted the body's need — food, comfort, the relief of physical suffering.

But note the framing: *"If you are the Son of God."* The temptation is not only about bread — it is about doubting identity. "If you really are who God said you are, prove it by using your power for yourself."

**Jesus' response:**

> "It is written: 'Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that comes from the mouth of God.'"
> — Matthew 4:4 (Deuteronomy 8:3)

The answer is Scripture. Not argument, not display of power, not compromise. **"It is written."**

### 2. The Temptation of the Eyes: The Kingdoms of the World

> "Again, the devil took him to a very high mountain and showed him all the kingdoms of the world and their glory. And he said to him, 'All these I will give you, if you will fall down and worship me.'"
> — Matthew 4:8–9

This is the temptation of **possession** and **power** — "you can have it all, without the Cross." Satan offers a shortcut to the Kingdom — without suffering, without death, without the long road of patient faithfulness.

**Jesus' response:**

> "Be gone, Satan! For it is written: 'You shall worship the Lord your God and him only shall you serve.'"
> — Matthew 4:10 (Deuteronomy 6:13)

There is no shortcut to the Kingdom that bypasses the Cross. Any offer of greatness that requires we bow to something other than God is the same temptation in different form.

### 3. The Temptation of Pride: Throw Yourself Down

> "Then the devil took him to the holy city and set him on the pinnacle of the temple and said to him, 'If you are the Son of God, throw yourself down, for it is written, "He will command his angels concerning you," and "On their hands they will bear you up."'"
> — Matthew 4:5–6

Satan now uses Scripture — misquoting Psalm 91. The temptation of pride: demand a miracle, test God's protection, turn faith into presumption.

**Jesus' response:**

> "Again it is written, 'You shall not put the Lord your God to the test.'"
> — Matthew 4:7 (Deuteronomy 6:16)

Scripture answers misquoted Scripture. The response to a twisted verse is always the full witness of the Word of God.

## The Three-Part Structure of Temptation: 1 John 2:16

> "For all that is in the world — the desires of the flesh and the desires of the eyes and pride of life — is not from the Father but is from the world."
> — 1 John 2:16

| 1 John 2:16 | The Three Temptations |
|------------|----------------------|
| Desires of the flesh | Turn stones to bread (body's cravings) |
| Desires of the eyes | Kingdoms and their glory (visual allure) |
| Pride of life | Throw yourself down (presumption, self-display) |

---

## Parallels: Adam and Eve vs. Christ

The first Adam fell to the same three temptations in the Garden:

| Garden (Genesis 3) | Wilderness (Matthew 4) |
|--------------------|------------------------|
| "The tree was good for food" (desires of the flesh) | "Turn stones to bread" |
| "It was a delight to the eyes" (desires of the eyes) | "All the kingdoms and their glory" |
| "Desired to make one wise" (pride of life) | "Throw yourself down" |

Adam took the fruit. Christ refused. As St. Paul writes: "The first man Adam became a living being; the last Adam became a life-giving spirit" (1 Cor 15:45). Christ reverses the fall in every detail.

---

## Old Testament Parallels

The pattern of temptation appears throughout the Old Testament:

| Figure | Temptation | Result |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Adam and Eve | Fruit — wisdom — pride | Fell |
| Israel in the wilderness | Food, testing God, idolatry | Fell (40 years) |
| David | Bathsheba (flesh), Uriah's death (power) | Fell, but repented |
| Saul | Pride, disobedience | Fell, rejected |
| Elijah | Discouragement after triumph | Fled, restored |

Christ, in the wilderness, corrects what all of them failed at — and does so by returning to the Word of God that they forgot.

## The Weapons: Fasting and Prayer

The tradition of the Church has always tied the battle against temptation to two practices:

**Fasting** — weakens the grip of the flesh's demands. When you can deny yourself bread, you can deny yourself other things. Fasting is training for the "turn stones to bread" moment.

**Prayer** — keeps you oriented toward God when temptation tries to reorient you toward the self. Jesus went into the wilderness after being prayed over and anointed at His baptism. He emerged victorious. His weapon the entire time was the Word of God.

---

## "For It Is Written" — The Weapon We Have

Three times Jesus answered: "It is written." Not feelings. Not experience. Not argument. The written Word of God — memorized, internalized, ready.

This is why Scripture memorization is not optional in the Christian life. In the moment of temptation, there is rarely time to look something up. The Word must already be inside.

> "I have stored up your word in my heart, that I might not sin against you."
> — Psalm 119:11

---

## How to Say No

Practical framework for facing temptation:

1. **Name it** — What category is this? Flesh, eyes, or pride?
2. **Find the lie** — Every temptation involves a false promise. What is it promising?
3. **Answer with Scripture** — "It is written..." Find the verse that addresses this specific temptation
4. **Fast** — Deny yourself in a related area. Fasting from food strengthens the will in all areas.
5. **Pray** — Bring the temptation into the light. Temptation loses power when it is named before God and another person.

---

## Encountering Christ in Temptation

The title of this lesson is intentional. When we are tempted and we resist by the weapons Christ gave us, we encounter Christ Himself:

> "Resist the devil, and he will flee from you. Draw near to God, and he will draw near to you."
> — James 4:7–8

The wilderness is not a place God abandons us. It is a place He meets us.
