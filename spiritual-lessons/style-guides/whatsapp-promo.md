# WhatsApp Promo Style Guide

This file defines how to write promotional messages for a Coptic Orthodox adult
meeting. The goal is messages that are warm, inviting, and spiritually compelling
without being cheesy, heavy-handed, or overly formal.

---

## Purpose

Short WhatsApp messages that encourage adults to attend the meeting.
They should make people feel: *this is worth showing up for.*

---

## Tone

- Warm and enthusiastic, not cold or obligatory
- Spiritually meaningful, not generic
- Readable at a glance
- Concise - people skim WhatsApp
- Inviting, not guilt-tripping
- Occasionally lightly playful - a smile is fine, a cringe is not

---

## Standard Structure

```
[Hook line with 1-2 emojis]

[1-3 short teaser lines about the lesson theme]

[Time and location]

[Invitation line]

[Optional: playful or smile-inducing closing line]
```

---

## Message Versions

Request any of these when asking for a promo:

### Concise (3 to 5 lines)
For groups that prefer brevity. Hook + one teaser + time/location + invite.

### Medium (full structure)
The standard version. All five elements above.

### Playful
Lighter tone. A gentle pun, a warm joke, or a line that makes someone smile.
Still spiritually grounded - not silly.

### Reverent
More serious and contemplative. For Holy Week, fasting seasons, or heavier topics.
No puns. Quieter energy. Still warm and inviting.

---

## Examples

### Concise
```
✝️ What does it look like when Christ meets us exactly where we are?

Join us Sunday as we dig into one of the most personal encounters in the Gospels.

📍 [Location] | 🕕 [Time]
All are welcome.
```

### Medium
```
🔥 38 years is a long time to wait.

But Christ didn't walk past the man at the pool.
And He doesn't walk past us either.

This Sunday we're looking at one of the most striking healing encounters in
John's Gospel - and what it reveals about the places in our own lives where
we've stopped expecting change.

📍 [Location] | 🕕 [Time]
Come as you are. Bring a friend.
```

### Playful
```
💸 Treasure hunt this Sunday - but not the kind you're thinking.

We're asking: where is your heart actually anchored?
Spoiler: the answer might be uncomfortable. The good kind of uncomfortable.

📍 [Location] | 🕕 [Time]
See you there 😄
```

### Reverent
```
✝️ This Sunday we enter the stillness of Holy Week together.

We will walk through the hours, the prayers, and the hymns of the Passion -
slowly, as the Church has always walked them.

Come ready to pray.

📍 [Location] | 🕕 [Time]
```

---

## What to Avoid

| Avoid | Why |
|---|---|
| "Don't miss this!" | Sounds like a sales pitch |
| "Amazing lesson this Sunday!" | Generic, says nothing |
| Long paragraphs | People don't read them |
| Guilt-based language | "You really should be there..." |
| Over-explaining the lesson | Tease it, don't summarize it |
| All caps for emphasis | Reads as shouting |
| Too many emojis | Looks cluttered |
| Overly formal language | Feels like a church bulletin, not a message from a friend |

---

## Practical Notes

- Always include time and location. People forget.
- "Bring a friend" is a simple, non-pressuring invite line that works well.
- If the lesson has a striking title, use it or tease it.
- If the lesson touches a universal struggle (fear, anger, disappointment, spiritual dryness),
  name that struggle in the promo - it creates immediate connection.
- For series: mention which session number it is so regular attendees can track continuity.

---

## Filling In Time and Location

The time and location are specific to each church and meeting.
Always replace the placeholder `[Location]` and `[Time]` with the actual values
before sending. Example:

```
📍 St. Mark Church, Fellowship Hall | 🕕 7:00 PM
```
